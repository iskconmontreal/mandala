## Greetings

  {greeting}, {firstName}
  {rotating line}
  Greeting styles to pick from randomly:

  A) Vaishnava greetings (traditional, short):

  "Hare Krishna" — the obvious one, never gets old
  "All glories to Srila Prabhupada" — sets the mood
  "Jaya" — celebratory, energetic ("Jaya, Dhirakanta!")

  B) Prabhupada's letter-style (warm, personal):

  "Please accept my humble obeisances" — but this is guru-to-disciple, wrong direction
  "I hope this meets you well" — adapted from his letters, works well
  "Thank you for your service" — he said this constantly

  C) Service-acknowledging (functional warmth):

  "Ready to serve" — simple, purpose-oriented
  "Your service matters" — affirming without flattery
  "Krishna sees every effort" — encouraging

  D) Time-aware (Vedic rhythm):

  Morning: "Early service is the sweetest"
  Day: "Steady in service"
  Evening: "Another day of service well spent"
